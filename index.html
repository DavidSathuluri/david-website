About
Background & commitments

I am a climate justice and AI governance researcher and policy advocate based in New York.[cite:0] My work brings together climate science, critical social theory, and technology studies to understand how AI systems interact with energy transitions, extractive infrastructures, and long histories of caste, race, and colonialism.[cite:0]

Trained at Columbia University, I write and collaborate across academic, journalistic, and policy spaces, working with movements and communities to challenge techno-solutionist narratives and insist on justice-centered approaches to climate and digital governance.[cite:0]

Research focus
Themes & questions
Core questions
  • ⯈How do AI and data infrastructures reshape climate and energy transitions, especially in the global South?
  • ⯈In what ways do caste, race, and class hierarchies get reproduced or contested through climate-tech governance?
  • ⯈What forms of labor, care, and extraction are hidden inside “green” and “smart” futures?
  • ⯈How can regulatory, civic, and worker-led interventions pre-empt AI-fuelled climate apartheid?
Areas of work
  • ◆AI in climate and energy planning, carbon accounting, and climate risk management.
  • ◆Caste, race, and environmental governance in South Asia and the diaspora.
  • ◆Labor, automation, and the politics of “green” and digital transitions.
  • ◆Ethics, regulation, and movements for democratic AI governance.
Current work. I am developing research and public writing on how AI-intensive climate governance risks producing new forms of climate apartheid — and how communities, workers, and regulators can resist these trajectories.[cite:0]
